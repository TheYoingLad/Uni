These are the sights that you have to see if you ever visit the city:

Alcazaba and Gibralfaro viewpoint: The Alcazaba is a palatial fortification in Málaga, Spain, built during the period of Muslim-ruled Al-Andalus. It is one of the best-preserved alcazabas in Spain. The Alcazaba is also connected by a walled corridor to the higher Castle of Gibralfaro, and adjacent to the entrance of the Alcazaba are remnants of a Roman theatre dating to the 1st century AD.

Botanical Garden: A must-see for every nature lover, La Concepción Historical-Botanical Garden (Jardín Botánico-Histórico La Concepción) is a true gem recognised not only in Malaga but throughout Europe. This English style garden hosts an impressive collection of tropical and subtropical plants embellished with multiple ponds, waterfalls and sculptures.

Málaga Old Town: The curious thing about Málaga is that all of its signature monuments are in an area known as the Historic Centre. The churches, convents and palaces holding a tremendous cultural and artistic value are on this group of mostly pedestrian streets with a Moorish air. Taking a walk around this area is pleasant with an atmosphere full of outdoor seating at bars and restaurants becoming the star of the show.
